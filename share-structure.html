The Charter Rules

What happens when
rules are broken.

The cultural charter is not a suggestion list. It is written into the company's Articles of Association. Breaking it has legal consequences — built into the share agreement you sign on entry.

⚔️

Physical Violence — Share Forfeiture

Any proven act of physical violence against another person or animal within city limits triggers the community tribunal process. If confirmed: the offender's Class-A share is forfeited. The company buys it back at 70% of current book value. The remaining 30% goes into the community welfare fund. The person and their family must vacate within 60 days.

This is non-negotiable. There is no second warning for violence. This is what makes the city safe.
🍖

Non-Vegetarian Activity — Eviction Notice

Bringing meat, fish, or eggs into the city; consuming non-vegetarian food on the premises; or attempting to slaughter any animal within the city boundaries results in a formal warning first, then share forfeiture on repeat offence.

The city is 100% vegetarian. It grows only plant food. It consumes only plant food. This is the agreement you sign.
🐄

Animal Harm — Immediate Review

Harming any animal within the city or ecological buffer — including domestic animals, native wildlife, or animals in the outer sanctuary ring — triggers immediate tribunal review. Outcome depends on severity: warning, fine paid to the welfare fund, or share forfeiture.

Ahimsa extends to every being. The animals here are protected by the same charter that protects you.
🚪

Voluntary Exit — Right of First Refusal

You can exit at any time by selling your shareblock. The company holds Right of First Refusal (ROFR) — it can buy back the share at current book value. If the company declines, you may sell to an approved buyer on the waitlist who signs the cultural charter. Resale prices are capped at: original price + inflation + verified home improvements. No speculative flipping.

You can always leave. But you cannot profit unfairly from this community's success at others' expense.
🧬

Inheritance — Full Transfer

On the death of a shareholder, the Class-A shareblock is inherited by designated family members who agree to the cultural charter. If no eligible heir exists or agrees, the company buys the share back at book value and places it back on the founding waitlist.

This is a generational asset — not just for you, but for your children and grandchildren.
Frequently Asked Questions

Simple answers to
common questions.

Is this the same as buying a flat? ▾
No. When you buy a flat, you own concrete walls. When you buy a Sūrya Nagari shareblock, you own 1/10,000th of 5,000 acres, a GPU campus, solar farms, factories, commercial offices, a hospital, and a school — plus a designated 3BHK home. Your share grows in value with the whole city, not just one apartment.
Can I rent my home out to someone else? ▾
No. Class-A shareblocks are for resident-owners only. You must live in the city to benefit from the services. If you are away for an extended period, your services continue but you cannot sub-let the home to non-shareholders. The city is a community of owners, not landlords and tenants.
What if the company makes a loss one year? ▾
The city builds a liquid endowment reserve (₹1,000 Cr) specifically for this. In lean years, the reserve covers city services and no dividends are paid that year. In strong years, excess is split between replenishing the reserve and shareholder dividends. Your home, food, health, and education are always protected by the reserve — dividends are the variable part.
Is this legal in India? ▾
Yes. The structure is modelled as a Private Limited Company under the Indian Companies Act, 2013, with elements of a Producer Company and Multi-State Co-operative Society. Land acquisition for townships of this scale requires state-level approval (NA conversion) and may use a Joint Venture model to navigate Land Ceiling Acts. We are working with legal advisors on the precise structure for Phase 1.
When can I buy shares? ▾
Not yet. We are in the vision and planning phase. Register your interest on the waitlist — when the founding shareholder window opens, waitlist members are first to be invited. No payment is taken at this stage.
Is this a religious community? ▾
No. Sanatan values (non-violence, truth, cleanliness, contentment) are ethical principles, not a religion. People of all faiths and backgrounds are welcome. The only requirements are: vegetarianism, non-violence, no animal harm, and commitment to the community. You do not need to follow any specific religion to live here.
Can I fork this project and build my own city? ▾
Yes — that is the entire point. Everything is open source under AGPL-3.0 + CC BY-SA 4.0 + CERN-OHL-S. Take the legal templates, the engineering plans, the governance protocols — modify them for your climate and culture — and build your own. Apply for "Official Sūrya Nagari Estate" certification if you want our audit and endorsement.
