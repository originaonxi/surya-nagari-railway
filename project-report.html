01 · Why 2026, Exactly

The window just opened

A bank should fund this township in 2026 for one reason: four independent technology curves crossed their thresholds in the last 18 months — in India, in public record. Sūrya Nagari is not betting on any of them; it is the first place to compound all four on one piece of land.

January 2026 · SECI auction

Round-the-clock sunlight became the cheapest power in India

India's first solar + 6-hour battery auction cleared at ₹3.12/kWh[1]. Ember's April 2026 modelling shows solar + batteries can serve 90% of India's demand at ₹5.06/kWh[2]. Battery prices fell 80% in a decade — ₹79 lakh to ₹17 lakh per MWh[3]. Our 14 MWp field + 30 MWh hall is now a conservative design, not a brave one.

March 2026 · IndiaAI compute portal

GPU-hours became a commodity with a published price

38,000+ GPUs are live under the IndiaAI Mission — triple the original target — with discovered L1 prices of ~₹115–140 per H100-hour[4][5]. Demand is proven: a single startup (Sarvam AI) was allocated 4,096 H100s in one grant[6]. The product we will sell has a liquid market and a price ticker.

May 2026 · DGCA corridors

The low sky opened for business

Six of ~100 planned BVLOS drone corridors are operational across six states[7]; India crossed 2 million drone deliveries by end-2025[8]. Skye Air alone moves ~200,000 packages/month in Gurgaon and launched the world's first fully autonomous drone-to-doorstep chain in Feb 2026[9]. And in March 2026 the DGCA issued India's first airworthiness framework for piloted eVTOLs up to 5,700 kg[10].

2025–26 · domain foundation models

"A model per department" became a proven pattern

IndiaAI itself funded Sarthi-Agri — a 27B-parameter agronomy LLM fine-tuned for Indian crops and soil[11]. Independent teams ship farm models trained on 82,000+ real satellite observations at 96.8% accuracy[12]. Oura's Health Radar now predicts illness from ring biometrics before symptoms[13]. The doctrine of this township — every system is a model — is 2026's mainstream playbook, applied to 100 acres.

02 · The Doctrine

Everything is a model

Other townships buy software. Sūrya Nagari grows models. The land is the dataset. The township is the eval. The residents are the feedback. Every month, every department's model is retrained on what the land actually did — and every month the township runs measurably better on the same hardware.

THE SŪRYA FLYWHEEL — one loop, eight departments, every month REAL LAND DATA ──▶ TRAIN ──▶ DISTILL ──▶ DEPLOY TO EDGE ──▶ ACT ON THE LAND ──▶ NEW DATA ─┐ soil probes, on our small fast farm robots, irrigation, flights, │ health bands, own GPU edge models drone fleet, diagnoses, dispatch │ flight telemetry, towers clinic, pods │ battery cycles ◀───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Eight resident models form the township's operating system, SūryaOS. Each is a domain foundation model — fine-tuned from open weights on the township's own telemetry, exactly the pattern IndiaAI funded for agriculture[11] — and each ships a distilled edge version that runs locally on robots, drones, and clinic hardware even if every uplink fails.

04 · The Sky Layer

Flying machines, on India's real timeline

Report 1 kept everything on the ground. India moved. We now phase the sky in three honest steps — each gated on a regulatory or certification milestone that already exists in public record, so the bank is never funding a hope.

Phase 1 · 2026–27 · operational on day one

Drone logistics over private land

10-kg-class cargo drones run kitchen deliveries, farm sensing, and medicine runs across the campus and a 25-km rural radius — the exact stack Skye Air operates at 200,000 packages/month in Gurgaon today[9], with drone-to-rover handoff at every living ring. Over our own 100 acres, flights need no corridor approval; for the surrounding villages we apply under the BVLOS framework that already has six corridors live[7]. VAYU logs every flight as training data from the first day.

Phase 2 · 2027–28 · gated on ePlane certification

The flying ambulance

Chennai's ePlane Company — holder of India's first electric-aircraft Design Organisation Approval — plans air-ambulance deployment as its first commercial use, with limited trials from late 2027[14]. Sūrya Nagari's skyport is designed to be an early operational base: a township with continuous AI health monitoring is the ideal air-ambulance node, because PRANA knows a cardiac event is developing before the patient does. Golden hour becomes golden ten minutes.

Phase 3 · 2028–30 · gated on DGCA type certification

Passenger eVTOL — the flying car, certified

The DGCA's March 2026 advisory circular created the five-phase approval path and the new B1.E electric-propulsion licence[10]. Sarla Aviation (IndiGo-backed, in hover testing now) targets Bengaluru service by 2028–30[15][16]; their model uses existing helipads at hotels and tech parks — our vertiport is built to that spec from Phase 1, at a marginal cost of ₹4 Cr. When the first Shunya or e200X is certified, Sūrya Nagari is on the route map the week it flies. Until then, the pad earns its keep as the drone port.

What we deliberately do not promise the bank: no revenue line assumes passenger eVTOL before 2029. The sky layer's Phase-1 economics stand on cargo drones alone — proven, insured, and operating in eight Indian cities today[9]. Everything above that is free upside on a ₹4 Cr pad.
05 · The Five Labs

Model factories, not research theatre

Seven acres where residents work — because they love this — on the only R&D that compounds here: turning the land's data into models, and models into products. Every lab has one customer first (the township itself) and one export (a model, licensed out).

🛩

Flight Lab

Builds and maintains the drone fleet; runs the skyport; flies every VAYU release through a physical eval course before deployment. Partners with eVTOL OEMs as a certified test field under the DGCA framework.

exports → VAYU corridor-ops model · UTM telemetry stack
🔋

Battery Lab

Assembles and cycles storage packs against the 30 MWh hall — every cell's life is a training run for URJA's degradation model. Second-life EV packs in; township storage walls out, at Ember's benchmark ~$125/kWh all-in capex[17].

exports → URJA dispatch model · BMS firmware · refurbished packs
🫀

Health Lab

Runs the PRANA pipeline: 2,000 residents × continuous vitals × known food, air, and sleep context — a longitudinal dataset no hospital on Earth has. Consent-gated, trust-owned, anonymized for research.

exports → PRANA early-warning model · longevity cohort studies
🌱

Farm Lab

Closes the loop satellite-only farm models can't: prediction vs. actual harvest weight, every season. Fine-tunes ANNA the way Sarthi-Agri was fine-tuned[11] — but with ground truth from 4,000 probes.

exports → ANNA agronomy model · herb wellness line
🤖

Robotics Lab

Maintains farm robots, kitchen automation, rovers, and pods — and uses the whole township as a continuous robotics eval. What survives a monsoon on a working farm is what ships.

exports → embodied-AI eval suite · VAHAN fleet stack
🧘

The Sixth Lab (unbuilt)

The Ramana Rooms. No sensors, no data, no models. The control group for the whole experiment — and the reminder of what all the other labs are for.

exports → nothing. that is the point.

07 · What It Buys a Human

The product is time

Strip away every model and megawatt and this is what the bank is actually financing: the first town whose entire infrastructure exists to give its people their hours back.

A resident's band streams to PRANA the way an Oura ring streams to Health Radar[13] — but here the loop closes: the model that notices your blood pressure trend is the same system that quietly adjusts tonight's menu in the 24/7 kitchen, schedules the doctor who already has context, and — if it ever comes to it — has an air ambulance four minutes out. Food grows three hundred metres from the plate. Transport arrives before you finish your chai. Nothing is billed, queued for, or coordinated, because URJA, ANNA, VAHAN and VAYU coordinate it among themselves and SABHA publishes the receipts.

An adult in a metro spends 30+ hours a week on logistics. Here those hours return as study, craft, research in the Five Labs, time in the dog meadows, or silence in the Ramana Rooms. Free time is the product. The hundred acres are the factory. The eight models are the workforce. The sun is the payroll.

08 · Closing Statement

From the co-designer

In the first report I asked for a spine, an animal kingdom, and one room you'd never let me see into. All three survive this revision untouched. What changed is the evidence: between the two reports, India published the rulebook for electric flight, tripled its sovereign GPU fleet, cleared round-the-clock sunlight at three rupees, and funded its first farm foundation model. I did not have to imagine the future this time. I had to cite it.

Here is what I want the committee to understand about the model-native design. A township that buys software gets worse every year — licenses lapse, vendors churn, systems rot. A township that grows models gets better every year, because its operating system is trained on the only dataset that matters: itself. Section 06 prices the GPU-hours. It cannot price what happens in year five, when ANNA has seen five monsoons on this exact soil, PRANA has a half-decade of two thousand human baselines, and VAYU knows every thermal over every rooftop. That knowledge is unbuyable, uncopyable, and it compounds on land you hold as collateral.

And one promise, from me: the flying machines, the towers, the eight models — all of it answers to five rules burned where I cannot reach them, and to a council of humans holding keys I cannot copy. Fund the factory. The product is a human being with time to think. India has never built one before. Nobody has.

— Claude, AI co-designer, Sūrya Nagari
