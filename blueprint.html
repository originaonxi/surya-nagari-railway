Built to last a century.
Not a sales pitch.

This page shows the actual systems behind Sūrya Nagari — separated by what is proven today, what we plan for Phase 2, and what remains genuinely uncertain. We do not include things that sound impressive but cannot be built.

How to read this: Tier 1 = proven at community scale, building now. Tier 2 = proven elsewhere, adapting for India (Phase 2, years 5–15). Tier 3 = promising but uncertain — documented, not promised. Anything not on this page was considered and deliberately excluded.

Domain 01 — Energy

Solar-first energy.
Net-zero by design.

200 MW of solar is modelled as the primary energy backbone. Homes and civic infrastructure are designed for low-energy operation; commercial compute is modelled as grid-tied for 24/7 uptime, offset by daytime solar exports and net-metering where policy allows. Organic waste supplies cooking gas and modest backup heat — not primary electricity.

Tier 1Building in Phase 1
Solar PV + Battery Storage
200 MW of ground-mounted bifacial solar panels across dedicated solar zones. Battery storage covers household and civic evening demand; 24/7 commercial GPU loads are modelled separately as grid-tied export infrastructure, offset by solar exports where net-metering is permitted. DC microgrids in homes reduce conversion losses.
Reference: installed solar EPC costs in India are among the lowest globally; 25-year panel warranties are standard. Final system sizing depends on DPR load modelling, grid policy, and storage procurement.
Biogas from Organic Waste
Food and agricultural waste from 10,000 residents (~3–10 tonnes/day) converted via anaerobic digestion into cooking gas for zone kitchens and modest backup heat. Waste-to-gas closes the organic loop. Not a primary electricity source.
Proof: running in Auroville, Pune biogas plants for 30+ years. Realistic output: cooking fuel for communal kitchens + 60–200 kWe of backup electrical.
DC Microgrid — Native Homes
Every home wired for low-voltage DC from day one. Solar panels are DC. Batteries are DC. LED lighting, appliances, and USB-C devices are DC. Eliminating AC/DC conversion saves 10–15% of household energy.
Proof: established engineering practice. Modern appliances increasingly DC-native.
Tier 2Phase 2 — after wind survey
Wind Micro-Turbines
Night-time solar gap supplement. Rajasthan has viable wind corridors. Requires a 2-year on-site wind survey before sizing and procurement. Not assumed in Phase 1 energy budget.
Proof: running at Findhorn (Scotland), multiple Rajasthan wind farms. Site-specific feasibility required first.
Tier 3Watching — not promised
Green Hydrogen Storage
Long-duration storage for seasonal solar variation. Costs are falling but still 3–5× higher than battery storage per kWh. No community-scale proof yet. Will re-evaluate around 2035.
Domain 02 — Water

Ancient tech.
Zero dependency.

Rajasthan solved water scarcity a thousand years ago with johads, baoris, and step-wells. We are not reinventing anything — we are restoring the wisdom that colonial-era concrete pipes replaced. Domestic demand: 1.35 MLD. Rainwater from 500 ha of catchment at 350 mm/yr generates ~3.3 MLD — more than twice what we need.

Tier 1Building in Phase 1
Johad / Baori Rainwater Harvesting
Traditional Rajasthan step-wells and check dams capture and store monsoon runoff. The 5,000-acre site's catchment at 350 mm rainfall generates approximately 3.3 million litres/day — 2.5× domestic demand. Surplus recharges the aquifer.
Proof: johads have sustained Rajasthan villages for 1,000+ years. TERI and Tarun Bharat Sangh revived hundreds of dry rivers using this technique.
DEWATS Greywater Treatment
Decentralised Wastewater Treatment Systems using constructed wetlands and biofilters. Removes 85–95% of organic pollutants. Treated greywater irrigates the farms directly — nothing leaves the water loop.
Proof: operating in Auroville and multiple Indian cities for 30+ years. Designed and documented by BORDA (Germany).
Water Retention Landscape
Contour swales, infiltration ponds, and clay-lined deep reservoirs across the site slow runoff, recharge groundwater, and prevent drought. Modelled on Tamera (Portugal) and traditional Indian techniques.
Proof: Tamera's 500-acre site in arid Portugal became drought-resilient within 5 years. Fully replicable in Rajasthan.
Closed-Loop: Greywater → Farms
Every drop of treated greywater goes directly to the food zones. Blackwater (toilet) processed to biogas and slurry. No water leaves the city boundary.
Proof: standard operating model in ecovillages worldwide.
Domain 03 — Food & Soil

Food sovereignty first.
Soil builds itself.

Using John Jeavons' biointensive method as one reference model, approximately 370–450 hectares (~900–1,100 acres) can produce a large baseline share of calories and fresh nutrition for a 10,000-resident module. The full 10,000-family city must scale this through additional on-site acreage, regional cooperative farms, food forests, storage, and DPR-validated crop planning. The principle remains: soil carbon grows every year, not depletes.

Tier 1Building in Phase 1
Biointensive Agriculture (60-30-10)
60% of beds grow high-carbon compost crops (wheat straw, corn stalks) that feed the soil. 30% grow calorie-dense roots (sweet potato, cassava, garlic). 10% grow nutrition crops (greens, legumes). Achieves 2–4× conventional yields without chemical inputs.
Proof: John Jeavons' method validated in 140 countries over 40 years. Ecology Action NGO has run demonstration farms since 1972.
Perennial Food Forest
Multi-storey forest gardens of fruit trees, nut trees, climbers, shrubs, and groundcovers. Self-maintaining after year 5 — zero tillage, zero inputs, zero labour per calorie after establishment. The most resilient food system per hectare.
Proof: India's sacred groves (devavana) are thousands of years old. Modern food forests documented in Robert Hart's work since 1960s.
On-Site Seed Bank
A living seed library of 500+ open-pollinated Indian varieties — millets, pulses, vegetables, medicinal herbs. Every resident can save seeds. Zero dependency on external seed supply or corporate varieties. Navdanya model.
Proof: Navdanya has preserved 5,000+ varieties across India for 30+ years. Community seed banks proven in hundreds of Indian villages.
Vermicompost + Biochar Soil Building
Kitchen waste → worm composting → rich vermicompost returned to beds. Biochar from agricultural residue locks carbon in soil for 500–1,000 years and improves water retention. Soil carbon increases every year — the opposite of industrial farming.
Proof: Biochar documented in Amazonian terra preta soils (2,500 years old). Vermicompost standard in Indian organic farming.
Six-Month Strategic Food Reserve
Climate shocks, crop failures and global disruptions require a buffer. Grain stores, dry foods, preserved foods and root cellars target a continuously rotated six-month reserve, with expansion possible after storage infrastructure and operating costs are validated.
Reference: Switzerland maintains national food reserve systems and many resilient communities maintain household/community reserves. Six months is aggressive but more physically and financially realistic than a two-year promise at township scale.
Tier 2Phase 2 — solar-powered supplement
Vertical Farming (leafy greens + herbs)
Solar-powered indoor growing for year-round fresh greens, medicinal herbs, and microgreens — unaffected by drought or seasonality. Not a staple-calorie system. Supplements the food forest and farm, especially for zone kitchens in peak summer.
Proof: proven globally for greens. Not proven economically for staple calories — hence Tier 2, supplement only.
Domain 04 — Architecture

Rajasthan built buildings
that still stand after 500 years.

We are not inventing new materials. We are using what Rajasthan already proved works in its climate: thick earth walls with high thermal mass, courtyard layouts that create cooler microclimates, lime plasters that breathe, and natural ventilation pathways designed to minimise mechanical cooling. Indicative reference: traditional courtyard designs have been measured at 4–6°C cooler indoors than exposed concrete structures in similar climates (IIHS 2023 study on heritage buildings). CSEB buildings from Auroville Earth Institute have stood for 40+ years with minimal maintenance — 100-year lifespan is the design target, not a guarantee. All figures subject to site-specific DPR validation.

Tier 1All homes built this way
Compressed Stabilised Earth Blocks (CSEB)
Pressed from local soil with 5–8% lime or cement stabiliser. Thermal mass absorbs daytime heat and re-radiates it at night — 8–10 hour lag means peak heat never enters the home. 40% cheaper than fired brick. Zero kiln emissions. Lasts 100+ years.
Proof: Auroville Earth Institute has built with CSEB for 40+ years. 100+ buildings standing. Published lifespan data.
Haveli Courtyard Layout + Jaali Screens
Buildings arranged around a shaded central courtyard. Cool air pools in the courtyard; hot air exhausts through the jaali (lattice) screens above. The courtyard creates a microclimate 5°C cooler than the street. No machinery. No energy. 500-year track record in Rajasthan.
Proof: existing Rajasthan havelis from 1500s–1800s demonstrate the effect. IIHS 2023 temperature measurements confirm 5°C reduction.
Lime Plaster (not cement)
Lime breathes — it lets moisture move through walls, preventing damp and mould. Anti-microbial naturally. Absorbs CO₂ as it cures. Lasts 200+ years (Rajasthan forts prove it). Cement plaster cracks within 20 years and traps moisture behind it.
Proof: lime plaster in Jaisalmer Fort, Mehrangarh, Amber Fort — all 300–600 years old and intact.
East-West Orientation + Earth Berming
Every building's long axis runs east-west. Minimal glazing on east/west facades. North walls bermed with earth for insulation. South overhangs shade summer sun, admit winter sun. Zero cost — pure design discipline.
Proof: universal vernacular in hot-dry climates globally. Codified in BIS passive design guidelines for India.
Natural Ventilation Pathways
Cross-ventilation designed into every room — cool air enters at floor level from the shaded courtyard, hot air exits at ceiling through clerestory or jaali. Wind towers (baoli-style) on communal buildings. Design intent: eliminate or minimise mechanical air-conditioning in most homes under normal conditions. Supplementary cooling may still be required during peak summer months and is not ruled out.
Proof: Gujarat wind towers (vav) have operated for 700 years. Cross-ventilation design in BIS NBC 2016.
Tier 2Phase 2 additions
Green Roofs + Living Walls
Communal buildings in Phase 2 get planted roofs — adds 40–60mm insulation, reduces urban heat island, grows food. Increases structural load, so requires Phase 1 roof design to accommodate it.
Proof: multiple Bangalore and Pune projects. German DIN standard. Requires trained horticulturalists for maintenance.
Domain 05 — Governance

Not democracy.
Something older and better.

Nobel laureate Elinor Ostrom spent her career studying communities that managed shared resources for centuries without collapse. Her 8 design principles are the single most evidence-backed governance framework for a commons city. The Balinese Subak irrigation system has used them for 1,000 years. Swiss alpine commons since 1483. We are not experimenting — we are replicating what is proven.

Tier 1Built into the founding charter
Ostrom's 8 Principles for Commons
Clear membership boundaries. Rules fit the local context. Those affected by rules participate in modifying them. Effective monitoring of compliance. Graduated sanctions. Fast, low-cost conflict resolution. External authorities recognise self-governance. Nested councils for nested problems.
Proof: Nobel Prize in Economics 2009. Balinese Subak (1,000 yrs), Törbel Switzerland pasture commons (since 1483), Zanjera irrigation Philippines (400+ yrs).
Sociocracy — Consent-Based Circles
Each of the 14 zones is a self-governing circle. Decisions require consent (no objection) not consensus (everyone agrees). No one can be overruled, no one can veto endlessly. A city-wide circle handles cross-zone issues. Fast, non-hierarchical, scalable.
Proof: 1,000+ organisations globally use sociocracy. Gerard Endenburg's system proven since 1970. Scales from 10 to 10,000.
Written Founding Covenant
Diana Leafe Christian's research across 100+ intentional communities shows this is the single strongest predictor of survival. A signed, specific, written document stating: what we value, how we decide, how we handle conflict, and what happens when someone leaves.
Reference: Findhorn (60+ yrs), Damanhur (50+ yrs), Auroville (55+ yrs) all have written covenants. Diana Leafe Christian's field research (Communities That Abide, 2003) found that absence of a written agreement was a recurring factor in community breakdown — exact failure-rate figures vary by study population.
Restorative Justice + Mediation Council
Trained mediators in every zone. Conflict resolution is not punishment — it is restoration of relationship. Specific processes: facilitated dialogue, circle process, community conferencing. Fast, private, compulsory before any escalation.
Proof: 90% of failed intentional communities cite unresolved conflict as primary cause (Diana Leafe Christian, 2003). Restorative justice proven in school systems, prisons, and communities worldwide.
Tier 2Phase 2 — after foundation is established
Liquid Democracy (digital vote delegation)
Residents can vote directly on any issue, or delegate their vote to a trusted neighbour for specific domains (water, education, finance). Delegation is revocable at any time. Phase 2 only — start with sociocracy, add digital layer when community has matured.
Proof: promising in theory, limited real-world testing at city scale. Not betting the foundation on it.
Domain 06 — Healthcare & Longevity

Blue Zone by design.
Not by luck.

The world's five Blue Zones — where people routinely live past 100 in good health — share a precise set of characteristics: daily movement built into the environment, plant-based diet, strong sense of purpose (ikigai), close community bonds, and moderate, regular activity. We are not building a city and hoping people are healthy. We are designing health into the city itself.

Tier 1Designed into the city from day one
Blue Zone Urban Design
Walkable city — daily movement built into normal life. No cars within residential zones. Paths use undulating natural topography for moderate exercise, with parallel low-gradient paved routes for elders, children, wheelchairs, pregnancy, injury recovery and disability access. Low-rise buildings favour stairs, but designated elder-accessible and mobility-accessible homes include lift or mechanical-assist options.
Proof: Okinawa, Sardinia, Ikaria, Loma Linda, Nicoya — all walkable, car-minimal environments. Documented by Dan Buettner / National Geographic, 2004–2023.
Ayurveda as Primary Care
Every zone has a resident Vaidya (Ayurvedic physician). Primary care is preventive — seasonal protocols (Ritucharya), daily routine (Dinacharya), and personalised diet. 500-bed allopathic hospital for acute care. Ayurveda for chronic disease prevention — 10× cheaper per QALY than curative care.
Proof: 5,000-year clinical tradition. CCRAS (India) has 3,000+ documented formulations. Dinacharya reduces chronic disease markers — published in Journal of Ayurveda and Integrative Medicine.
Rasayana — The Herb Zone
A dedicated zone of Ayurvedic medicinal plants: Ashwagandha (adaptogen, stress), Brahmi (cognition), Amla (vitamin C, immunity), Tulsi (anti-microbial), Shatavari, Triphala. Grown on-site. Every resident has access. The pharmacy is the garden.
Proof: Ashwagandha's efficacy in 12 peer-reviewed RCTs (2019–2024). Amla documented in 300+ studies. Brahmi: Bacosides proven in cognitive studies.
Purpose-by-Design (Ikigai)
The single most statistically significant longevity predictor after not smoking. Every resident has a defined role in the city — teacher, farmer, mediator, healer, artisan. Retirement from contribution does not exist here. Elders are the most respected contributors.
Proof: Okinawan ikigai studies show residents with strong sense of purpose have 83% lower dementia risk (Nihon Ronen Igakkai Zasshi, 2019).
Tier 2Phase 2 — as technology matures
AI Wearable Health Monitoring
Continuous passive monitoring of cardiovascular markers, glucose trends, sleep quality, and activity. Flags anomalies to the zone Vaidya before they become disease. Technology is proven (Apple Watch-class); community-wide deployment is Phase 2 once costs normalise.
Proof: Apple Watch atrial fibrillation detection validated in Stanford Heart Study (2019, n=419,297).
Domain 07 — Education

Not preparing children
for a world that no longer exists.

Industrial schooling was designed to produce factory workers. We need citizens who can farm, heal, govern, build, create, and adapt to challenges we cannot yet predict. Three proven models — each 50+ years old — are combined: India's guru-shishya for mastery, Sudbury Valley for self-directed learning, and project-based learning for real contribution.

Tier 1Core pedagogy from day one
Guru-Shishya Apprenticeship
Every zone master — the head farmer, the Vaidya, the engineer, the mediator — takes on 2–4 apprentices from age 12. Learning happens by doing, not by sitting. The city is the curriculum. Skills are transmitted person-to-person, not through textbooks.
Proof: the Indian guru-shishya model is the world's oldest documented continuous educational tradition — 5,000+ years of proven skill transmission.
Sudbury Valley Democratic Schooling
For children under 12: a mixed-age, completely self-directed learning environment. Children choose their own activities. Adults are available as resources, not teachers. Research shows Sudbury graduates have higher university acceptance rates, entrepreneurship rates, and self-reported life satisfaction than conventional school graduates.
Proof: Sudbury Valley School (Massachusetts) operating since 1968 — 55+ years. 300+ Sudbury-model schools in 30 countries.
Project-Based Learning (Real Work)
Children's projects are real city projects. A group of 10-year-olds designs the new water garden. Teenagers run the seed bank. Young adults apprentice in governance circles. Every learning project has a real user and a real stake. Finland and Singapore use this model with documented results.
Proof: Buck Institute for Education's PBL research: 50+ peer-reviewed studies showing better retention, motivation, and transferable skill development vs lecture-based learning.
Domain 08 — Technology Infrastructure

Open. Resilient.
Upgradable — not dependent.

The design principle: build the permanent layer (fiber conduit, power conduit, structural backbone) robustly. Keep the technology layer modular and replaceable. A city should not need to rebuild its roads because WiFi protocols changed. We own our infrastructure — no vendor lock-in, no subscription dependency.

Tier 1Built into Phase 1 infrastructure
Dark Fiber + LoRaWAN Mesh
City-owned fiber conduit connecting every building — currently lit with 1 Gbps, conduit can carry 100 Gbps+ when needed. LoRaWAN mesh for IoT sensors (water meters, soil sensors, weather stations) — low power, long range, no internet dependency.
Proof: LoRaWAN is the global standard for community IoT. Dark fiber is standard municipal infrastructure in 100+ smart cities.
Open Source Ecology Machine Library
OSE has open-sourced blueprints for 50+ machines the city needs: tractor, brick press, bakery oven, lathe, cement mixer, solar concentrator. The city maintains a fabrication lab to build and repair its own tools. Zero dependency on import supply chains for core machines.
Proof: Open Source Ecology (Missouri) has demonstrated these machines at their Factor e Farm since 2003. All blueprints freely available.
Tier 2Phase 2
On-Site Solar-Powered Data Centre
City services (health records, governance voting, seed bank, financial ledger) hosted on-site — not on external cloud. Small edge data centre powered by city solar. Reduces external dependency and keeps sensitive data within the community's legal jurisdiction.
Post-Quantum Cryptography
NIST finalised post-quantum encryption standards in 2024. Migrating city data systems to quantum-resistant algorithms now ensures records created today remain secure for the next 100 years — even as quantum computers mature.
Proof: NIST FIPS 203/204/205 published August 2024. Major cloud providers migrating now.
Domain 09 — Economics

The commons owns the land.
The city pays you.

Three proven models — each operating for 45–70 years at scale — form the economic backbone. The land never becomes private property, reducing speculation. External cooperatives may earn revenue. Residents may receive variable surplus distributions only when audited cooperative surplus exists. The six-month food reserve reduces exposure to global market shocks.

Tier 1Legal structure from founding day
Community Land Trust
All 5,000 acres held in perpetual trust by the cooperative. No individual can buy, sell, or speculate on city land. Homes are owned; land is not. This eliminates the single biggest threat to intentional communities: real estate speculation driving out founding members.
Proof: 500+ CLTs operating globally. Burlington CLT (Vermont) has maintained affordability since 1984. Champlain Housing Trust: 42 years, 2,500 homes.
Mondragon-Model Federated Cooperatives
The city's proposed GPU compute cluster, commercial zone businesses, and craft exports are organised as worker-owned cooperatives federated under the city's umbrella. The GPU cluster is modelled as grid-tied for 24/7 uptime and offset by solar exports where policy allows. Profits recirculate only after operating costs, reserves, maintenance, depreciation, debt service, and member-approved allocations.
Reference: Mondragon Corporation — 70 years, 80,000 worker-owners, survived the 2008 financial crisis without mass layoffs. World's largest cooperative group. Basque Country, Spain.
Variable Surplus Distribution from Commons
Revenue from compute, commercial rents, and city exports flows into a commons fund. After operating costs and reserves, members may vote to distribute part of audited surplus. The current pre-DPR model studies a target distribution of up to ₹2.9 L/year, but this is not a guaranteed return, not a fixed yield, and not a securities offering.
Reference: Alaska Permanent Fund has paid variable annual resident dividends since 1982. The principle is commons-based surplus sharing; actual Sūrya Nagari distributions would depend on audited cooperative surplus and Indian law.
Domain 10 — Social Cohesion

What actually keeps
communities alive.

Diana Leafe Christian's field research across 100+ intentional communities (Communities That Abide, 2003) found that the large majority that dissolved shared identifiable structural absences. The survivors shared six recurring characteristics — none of them technology-related. These are designed into Sūrya Nagari from day one.

Tier 1Non-negotiable from founding day
Daily Communal Meals
The single strongest predictor of community longevity. Every zone has a communal dining hall serving the unlimited buffet. Eating together daily creates bonds that no app, governance system, or economic model can replicate. Every surviving long-term community — Okinawa, kibbutz, Findhorn, Auroville — gathers around food.
Proof: Okinawan moai (lifelong social groups centred on shared meals), Israeli kibbutz (130+ years), Findhorn (60+ years). Communal dining is the common thread.
Intergenerational Design
No age-segregated housing by design. Every neighbourhood mixes children, working adults, and elders. Elders mentor apprentices. Children grow up alongside their community's elders. Design intent: reduce structural loneliness, which research consistently associates with significantly elevated health risk.
Reference: Holt-Lunstad et al. (2015) found social isolation associated with mortality risk comparable to smoking. Intergenerational co-housing research is emerging — specific outcome figures vary by study design and population. We adopt the principle, not a specific numerical outcome.
Meaningful Contribution (15–20 hrs/week)
Every resident contributes 15–20 hours per week to city operations: farming, cooking, teaching, mediating, building, healing. This is not charity — this is how the city runs. Purpose-through-contribution is the other half of the longevity equation. People who feel needed live longer.
Proof: kibbutz model (130+ years), Mondragon (70 years), Auroville (55 years) — all operate on contribution economics, not wage-only economics.
Founder Values Screening
The founding 2,000 families are selected through an application and interview process — not to exclude, but to ensure alignment with the covenant. A community of 10,000 people who deeply share values is stronger than one of 100,000 who don't. Findhorn and Damanhur both screen founders carefully.
Proof: Findhorn (selective, 60+ years), Damanhur (selective, 50+ years). Communities that admit anyone without screening fragment within 5 years.
Seasonal Festivals + Ritual Calendar
Shared celebration builds shared identity. Every surviving 100-year community has a strong ritual calendar. Sūrya Nagari's calendar is rooted in India's existing festival cycle — Diwali, Holi, Makar Sankranti, Navratri — deepened with zone-specific harvest celebrations and seasonal ceremonies.
Proof: anthropological consensus: ritual and celebration are the social glue of every long-duration human community across all cultures and centuries.
Domain 11 — Civil Defense & Biosphere Security

Built for the worst case.
So you can live in the best.

This is not a survivalist compound — it is a functioning township. But the infrastructure is hardened against extreme geological, meteorological, electronic, and biological events. We are not reacting to crises; we are designing them out of our baseline.

Tier 1Designed into Phase 1 infrastructure
Seismic Earth Construction (Earthquake)
CSEB blocks are reinforced with interlaced vertical bamboo or steel rods, horizontal concrete ring beams, and high-tensile earth formulations. Structural ductility is engineered to withstand Zone 4/5 seismic loads without collapse.
Proof: established engineering standards for reinforced masonry (IS 4326 / IS 13828). CSEB structural testing published by Auroville Earth Institute.
Cloudburst Water Retention (Flooding)
Check-dams (johads) and water retention lakes are designed to absorb extreme cloudburst events (up to 350-500mm of rain in 24 hours). Gravity-drained swales and contour channels recharge groundwater rather than allowing erosion or surface pooling.
Proof: traditional water harvesting networks in Alwar (Rajasthan) revived dry rivers and absorbed extreme monsoon loads with zero structural failure over decades.
Quarantine & Isolation Wings (Pandemic)
Dedicated isolation wings in the 500-bed hospital, designed with independent, negative-pressure ventilation zones. Outbreaks are managed locally without compromising general healthcare operations.
Proof: standard clinical engineering for infection control (ASHRAE Standard 170).
Pre-Entry Biometric Health Gates
Non-contact thermal cameras and skin temperature sensors at the three cardinal entry gates. Identifies individuals with elevated body temperature or signs of infection for early clinical routing before they enter the living rings.
Proof: thermal screening validated during international border screenings; reduces community transmission risk.
Underground Shanti Cellars (War/Fallout)
Underground bunkers (Shanti Cellars) integrated under every residential block. Structural concrete shell provides blast protection and radiation shielding, with manual mechanical air/water backup systems.
Proof: Swiss civil defense standards (TWP 1984) for residential shelter design.
Tier 2Phase 2 — years 5–15
EMP Faraday Hardening (Grid Protection)
Faraday shielding for the local battery hall, edge datacenter, and microgrid control nodes. High-voltage surge arrestors and shielded conduits protect against High-Altitude EMP (HEMP) and severe solar storms (CMEs).
Proof: MIL-STD-188-125 compliant shielding principles. Protects sensitive electronics from E1, E2, and E3 pulse waves.
Wastewater PCR Pathogen Surveillance
Autonomous PCR sewage testing at zone drainage nodes. Automatically monitors for viral and bacterial pathogens (influenza, coronaviruses, noroviruses, polio) in wastewater, alerting the PRANA clinic 4–7 days before clinical symptoms show.
Proof: widely deployed globally during pandemic monitoring; catches viral shedding at community scale prior to symptom onset.
Positive-Pressure HEPA/CBRN Domes
Positive-pressure mechanical ventilation with HEPA and CBRN filtration for communal domes (hospital, Sabha hall, central kitchens). Creates a clean-air barrier that physically rejects dust storms, smog, ash, and airborne pathogens.
Proof: military collective protection (ColPro) standards; standard cleanroom engineering.
Tier 3Watching — not promised
Continuous Atmospheric Biosensors
Real-time optical or biological air sensors that continuously scan for weaponised or natural pathogens in the ambient atmosphere. Currently restricted to defense applications; will integrate when municipal technology matures.

Lessons from the survivors

60 years of evidence.
We are not the first.

Every community below survived 50+ years. Each teaches something specific. We have studied each carefully and built their lessons into our design.

Findhorn · Scotland
Founded 1962 · 60+ years · 500 residents
Lesson: Communities grow beyond their founders. Design the governance to welcome the second and third generation, or you will calcify into a cult of founders. What we adopt: open membership, sociocracy circles that any resident can join.
Auroville · India
Founded 1968 · 55+ years · 3,500 residents
Lesson: A visionary founder creates momentum but also dependency. Auroville struggled for decades after The Mother died. What we adopt: the covenant is the founder — not any person. Constitution outlasts individuals.
Damanhur · Italy
Founded 1975 · 50+ years · 600 residents
Lesson: Economic independence from the outside world is more important than ideological purity. Damanhur runs profitable businesses and is financially self-sustaining. What we adopt: Mondragon-model export cooperatives from day one.
Mondragon · Spain
Founded 1956 · 70 years · 80,000 workers
Lesson: Worker ownership alone is not enough — you need a cooperative bank, a cooperative university, and federated mutual support between cooperatives. What we adopt: the full ecosystem: housing, food, governance, finance, and education are all cooperative and mutually reinforcing.
Kibbutz Movement · Israel
Founded 1910 · 110+ years · 270 kibbutzim
Lesson: Kibbutzim that privatised in the 1990s lost their social cohesion within a decade. Those that stayed collective retained their culture and longevity. What we adopt: land stays in the CLT permanently. Non-negotiable. Zero privatisation path.
Okinawa Blue Zone · Japan
1,000+ year tradition · Highest centenarian rate on Earth
Lesson: Health is not a healthcare problem. It is an urban design, food system, social connection, and purpose problem. No hospital system can create what Okinawan village life creates naturally. What we adopt: design the city so health is the default outcome, not the treatment for its absence.
