01 · The Principle of the Baseline

Removing Survival Anxiety

In modern capitalism, a human is a customer. You buy or rent a home, and then pay utility companies, agricultural conglomerates, insurance corporations, and educational institutions forever. You must work to pay bills; you must pay bills to survive.

The Satyug Collective inverts this dynamic. By co-owning the city's infrastructure through a Multi-State Cooperative structure, the resident-shareholder pre-funds their survival. The 200 MW solar plant, the 1,500 acres of farms, the water microgrids, and the hospital are communal capital assets. They generate and distribute resources directly to residents at zero monthly cost.

"By automating survival, we release human consciousness from the daily struggle for physical existence. Survival is the baseline; creation is the goal."

03 · Economy

The SatyugCoin & Quota System

The city's internal economy separates basic survival from aspirational luxury using a **Dual-Tier Resource Model**.

Tier 1: The Sovereign baseline (Covered)

Housing, clean energy, organic food (prepared in community kitchens), K-University education, healthcare, fiber internet, EV transit, and home repairs require zero SatyugCoins. They are pre-funded by infrastructure capital.

Tier 2: The Aspiration Ledger (SatyugCoins)

To access luxuries — premium brands at the central mall, theater performances, artisanal cafes, Ayurvedic spas, and specialized imports — residents spend SatyugCoins.

Earning SatyugCoins: Residents earn coins by contributing their time to the city (teaching, medical care, farm assistance, maintenance, or writing). All earnings are recorded on the public blockchain, with care and education roles compensated highly to prevent standard wealth disparity.

The Quota System: To prevent capital concentration, access to limited luxury items (like theater box seats or specialty dining) is bound by time-based quotas rather than raw coin balance, ensuring every member has equal access to comfort.

04 · Civil Defence

Subterranean Sanctuaries (The Swiss Model)

True peace of mind requires structural safety. The Satyug Collective adopts the Swiss model of **100% civilian shelter coverage**, integrated directly into daily life.

◈
The Shanti Cellar: Under every home is a private, reinforced bunker. Lined with mycelium and clay for an organic feel, featuring micro-LED skylights to simulate natural light, it serves in peacetime as a soundproof meditation room or creative studio.
◈
The Garbha: A central subterranean sanctuary for 50,000 people. Caverns supported by basalt arches, lit by bioluminescent moss and solar tubes. Contains hot springs, theaters, communal dining, and the city archives to sustain community life during any extended threat.
◈
Threat Automation: SatyugShield AI monitors global natural and geopolitical indicators. If a threat is validated, the city transition to safe mode is coordinated automatically and calmly, accompanied by clear displays and acoustic guidance.

06 · Legal & Compliance

Legal & Financial Disclaimers

The Satyug Collective project represents a conceptual urban blueprint and civilizational design. It is not an active financial offering, nor is it registered under RERA or SEBI as a securities investment. Please review the following key structural parameters:

1. Cooperative vs. Private Limited Structure

Under the Indian Companies Act, 2013, a Private Limited Company is restricted to a maximum of 200 shareholders. Therefore, The Satyug Collective cannot operate as a standard Private Limited entity. The proposed legal structure is a Multi-State Co-operative Society (MSCS), which allows an unlimited number of member-shareholders. Each member holds one Class-A Membership Share providing residency rights and one vote in the general assembly.

2. Property Financing & Banking Regulations

Because land and buildings are held in perpetuity by the cooperative trust, individual members do not hold freehold titles to their residences. Consequently, traditional retail mortgages are not available. Financing must be secured via cooperative banking networks, peer-to-peer community credit unions, or pre-funded capital.

3. Price Capping & Anti-Speculation Covenants

To prevent speculative property bubbles, the cooperative bylaws cap the resale value of Class-A Membership Shares. Resale values are calculated strictly based on the original entry price adjusted for national inflation indices plus verified cost of home improvements. The cooperative retains the Right of First Refusal (ROFR) to buy back any exiting member's share.

4. Dispute Resolution & Mandatory Share Sale

Bylaws regarding vegetarianism, animal harm, and non-violence are contractually bound under the leasehold covenant. Any proven act of physical violence, as adjudicated by the elected Panchayat Council and the community tribunal, results in a **mandatory orderly sale** of the offender's share back to the cooperative treasury at 70% of current book value, with the remaining 30% deposited into the community welfare fund.
